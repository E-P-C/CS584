\section{How to include figures and tables}
\subsection{Including Figures in LaTeX}
To include a figure in LaTeX, you need to first upload a figure in  fire 'fig' and thenuse the `figure` environment and the `\verb|\includegraphics|` command from the `graphicx` package. Here's an example:

\begin{figure}[h]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.5\linewidth]{fig/tree.png}
    \caption{Figure caption.}
    \label{fig:ablation_study}
\end{figure}


The `\verb|\includegraphics|` command specifies the image file, and the `width` parameter adjusts the size. The `caption` command adds a caption below the figure, and `label` assigns a reference for citing.
The \verb|[h]| option specifies the preferred location for placing the figure. The letter 'h' meaning the figure should appear at the current location in the document. Other common placement options include 't', which places the figure at the top of the page, and 'b', which places it at the bottom of the page.
\subsection{Including Tables in LaTeX}

Tables in LaTeX are created using the `table` and `tabular` environments. Here's an example:

\begin{table}[h]
        \small
	\centering
	\caption{Table caption. You can add more rows or columns accordingly.}
	\label{tab:dataset}
	\begin{tabular}{clccc}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Task} & \textbf{Item} & \textbf{Train} & \textbf{Dev} & \textbf{Test}  \\
		\midrule
		\multirow{4}{*}{\textbf{data 1}} & item 1 & 8 & 1 & 15 \\
		& item 2 & 1 & 2 & 13 \\
		& item 3 & 5.7 & 5 & 0 \\
		& item 4 & 500 & 510 & 502 \\
		\midrule
		\multirow{4}{*}{\textbf{data 2}} & item 1 & 2 & 20 & 12 \\
		& item 2 & 70 & 30 & 71 \\
		& item 3 & 12.0 & 13.0 & 15.0 \\
		& item 4 & 0 & 10 & 20 \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The `tabular` environment defines the table structure, while the `|c|c|c|` specifies column alignment and vertical lines. Captions and labels can also be added to tables similarly to figures. Same to the figure, you can choose where you want to place them
